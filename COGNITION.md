# COGNITION

TACHYRIUM is the bounded cognition plane of the Verifrax stack.

It exists to transform ambiguity into typed, reviewable, machine-legible subordinate outputs without becoming constitutional law, accepted epoch truth, canonical world-state, authority-of-record, governed execution, terminal recognition, terminal recourse, or final verification truth.

## Lawful cognition role

TACHYRIUM may:

- classify subjects and surfaces
- detect contradictions and ambiguities
- generate candidate structures
- map lawful routing targets
- summarize bounded topology
- produce subordinate briefings for human or downstream review

TACHYRIUM may not:

- author constitutional law
- mutate accepted epoch truth
- hold canonical world-state
- issue authority objects
- authorize governed execution
- emit final verification verdicts
- recognize claims of record
- assign burden, remedy, escalation, or closure of record

## Input boundary

TACHYRIUM may read from adjacent sovereign surfaces, including:

- SYNTAGMARIUM for law and claim-class doctrine
- ORBISTIUM for accepted epoch and canonical state inputs
- AUCTORISEAL for authority publication inputs
- CORPIFORM for execution and receipt inputs
- VERIFRAX for verification-result and evidence inputs
- ANAGNORIUM for recognition inputs
- REGRESSORIUM for recourse inputs

Reading those surfaces does not transfer their sovereignty into TACHYRIUM.

## Output boundary

Lawful TACHYRIUM outputs are subordinate cognition artifacts only.

They may include:

- briefings
- candidate objects
- classification summaries
- contradiction analyses
- topology summaries
- routing candidates
- cognition outputs
- cognition audit records

They must remain reviewable as subordinate cognition and must not be misread as law, accepted truth, authority, execution legitimacy, recognition, recourse, or final verification.
